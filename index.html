My Road to Software Engineer

Hi! I'm Sherry Bai, an aspiring full-time Python developer. My journey began in the bustling world of Wall Street as a financial analyst. It was an interesting start, but I often found myself caught up in repetitive tasks.

Then there comes the magic day when I wrote my first VB program in an Excel spreadsheet in 2016, which saved me an hour daily. I felt like a magician, unlocking a new realm of possibilities.

Driven by this revelation, I immersed myself in the world of Python and SQL, focusing on streamlining workflows and enhancing data analysis processes. My efforts soon led me to a rewarding role as a data analyst in 2018. Over the next five years, I ascended through the ranks to become the lead data analyst for Arknights, a game with a global user base of 10 billion. In addition, I devoted my spare time to developing websites, web crawlers, and indie games.

This year marks a pivotal transition for me as I venture into full-time Python development. I'm currently seeking new opportunities and am eager to connect with like-minded professionals and potential employers. Feel free to reach out to me at sherrybaibai@163.com!

Skills

Where possible, we would like to see mostly CSS 1 & 2 usage. CSS 3 & 4 should be limited to widely-supported elements only, or strong fallbacks should be provided. The sherry-body is about functional, practical CSS and not the latest bleeding-edge tricks viewable by 2% of the browsing public. The only real requirement we have is that your CSS validates.

Luckily, designing this way shows how well various browsers have implemented CSS by now. When sticking to the guidelines you should see fairly consistent results across most modern browsers. Due to the sheer number of user agents on the web these days — especially when you factor in mobile — pixel-perfect layouts may not be possible across every platform. That’s okay, but do test in as many as you can. Your design should work in at least IE9+ and the latest Chrome, Firefox, iOS and Android browsers (run by over 90% of the population).

We ask that you submit original artwork. Please respect copyright laws. Please keep objectionable material to a minimum, and try to incorporate unique and interesting visual themes to your work. We’re well past the point of needing another garden-related design.

This is a learning exercise as well as a demonstration. You retain full copyright on your graphics (with limited exceptions, see submission guidelines), but we ask you release your CSS under a Creative Commons license identical to the one on this site so that others may learn from your work.
